Quantum finite automata:survey, status and research directions

Posted on 2022-03-15 | In quantum automata |

Quantum computing is concerned with computer technology on the principles of quantum mechanics,
with operations performed on the quantum circuits relying upon the quantum phenomenon of entanglement and superposition.

Quantum finite automata blend quantum mechanics with classical finite automata,
which is subject to the classical outcomes of quantum measurements.

History of Quantum Computing

  • 1961, Landauer ariculated a concept of reversibility in quantum computing.
  • 1982, Feyman initially proposed the idea of quantum computing after performing a quantum mechanics simulation on a classical computer.
  • 1985, Deutsch described a quantum Turing machine and determined the certainty of a universal quantum Turing machine based on the Church-Turing-Deutsch principle.
  • 1993, Yao determined that if a particular function exists(computable in polynomial time on a quantum Turing machine), then a polynomial-size quantum circuit can be designed for the function.
  • 1994, Shor designed a quantum algorithm for factoring a large integer n with space complexity $O(logn)$ and time complexity $O((logn)^2*log(logn))$.
  • 1994, Shor introduced the concept of the first quantum error correction code,encoding the information of 1 qubit into a highly entangled state of 9 qubits.
  • 1996, Grover proposed a quantum algorithm for searching for a single target in an unstructured database of size $n$ in $\sqrt{n}$ operations approximately.
  • The concept of quantum automata was first introduced by Moore and Crutchfield in 1997 and Watrous in 2000 respectively.

Classification of Quantum Automata

Quantum automata can be classified as:

  1. quantum finite automata
    • one-way quantum finite automata
      • measure-once one-way finite automata(MO-1QFA)
      • measure-many one-way finite automata(MM-1QFA)
      • enhanced one-way quantum finite automata(E1QFA):
        • pure states -> density operators
      • quantum finite automata with classical states:
        • 1QFAC and 1QCFA
        • 2QFAC and 2QCFA
    • two-way quantum finite automata
    • 1.5-way quantum finite automata
  2. quantum sequential machine
  3. quantum pushdown automata
  4. quantum Turing machine
  5. orthomodular lattice-valued automata
